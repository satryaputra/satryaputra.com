## Chapter 1: How a missing logo started everything

Back in 2024, when I used to build multiple projects,  
I kept getting stuck at the same exact problem every time.

finding a logo that actually suited my brand.

one of those projects was a ui library.

<FramedImage src="/images/blog/ui-library-sample.png" alt="UI Library Sample" />

I had built it to around **70% completion**.

- the code was solid
- the features worked
- everything was almost ready

except one thing… it had **no logo**.

I spent days trying to design something that matched the vibe of the product.

- tried tools
- tried templates
- tried generators

nothing felt right.

and because of that, the project slowly stopped moving forward.

that frustration stayed with me.

> ps: I was literally thinking of competing with shadcn with that ui library.

## Chapter 2: Building without knowing everything

When I started building [draftlogo](https://www.draftlogo.com),  
I honestly didn't know what a saas product really was.

I had no clear idea about:

- how saas work
- how payments work
- how authentication work

my mindset was simple:

> “I'll figure it out once I start.”

that's when things got interesting.

I didn't know [postgresql](https://www.postgresql.org/).  
I didn't know [prisma](https://www.prisma.io/).  
I didn't know [better-auth](https://www.better-auth.com/).

I had only heard about them from other builders.

so what was next?

I learned them.  
one by one.

and then I started implementing them into [draftlogo](https://www.draftlogo.com),  
slowly, like it was nothing.

what felt overwhelming at first  
started making sense over time.

> sometimes, you don't need full clarity.
> you just need to start
> and figure things out along the way.

## Chapter 3: The delay nobody talks about

Finally, it was time.

the moment I was waiting for.

my **first-ever saas product**  
and the first product I built on my own.

but here's the interesting part.

the product was only live on my **local machine**,  
waiting to be shared with people who didn't even know who I was.

the reason?

**dodo payments.**

I had to postpone my launch **three times**  
because my [dodo account was under verification](https://x.com/ratneshchipre/status/2017214133201191077?s=20).

- bank verification
- product verification
- the usual process

it felt frustrating, especially when the product was ready.

then one day, I got the email.

<FramedImage
  src="/images/blog/dodo-verification-email.png"
  className="w-full"
  alt="Dodo Verification Email"
/>

**verification complete.**

I instantly locked in.

I decided to launch [draftlogo](https://www.draftlogo.com) first on [X](https://x.com/ratneshchipre/status/2018316034512695494?s=20).

fun fact:

at the same time I was launching my product,  
my semester results were out.

I was launching a saas  
while my friends were discussing the results on discord.

surreal moment.

## Chapter 4: The response I didn't expect

To be honest,
I was expecting a better response initially.

especially because I had spent time learning [after effects](https://x.com/ratneshchipre/status/2014960889204793591?s=20)  
just to create a proper launch video for [draftlogo](https://www.draftlogo.com).

the response on [X](https://x.com/ratneshchipre/status/2018316034512695494?s=20) was decent.

but everything changed when I launched on [peerlist launchpad](https://peerlist.io/ratneshchipre/project/draftlogo).

that one week was special.
really special.

some moments I'll always remember:

- Akash Bhadange (co-founder & ceo of peerlist) upvoted [draftlogo](https://www.draftlogo.com)
- made it to the [top 100 builders on peerlist](https://x.com/ratneshchipre/status/2020796548603400250?s=20)
- 50 upvotes by day 3
- [100 upvotes](https://x.com/ratneshchipre/status/2022324042339131612?s=20) by day 5
- [draftlogo](https://www.draftlogo.com) became **#3 project of the week**

<FramedImage src="/images/blog/project-of-week.png" alt="Project of the Week" />

I never imagined that.

these were the stats so far:

- **450** visitors
- **1,197** page views
- users from **45+ countries**

people were actually using the product.

they were loving it.  
they were sharing feedback.

and honestly,  
I'm really proud of what I built.

but the journey isn't over yet.

my next goal?

> earning my first dollar from my first saas.
